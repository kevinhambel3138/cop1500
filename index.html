About Me

I learned how to use a computer at the age of 4 and since then my fascination with technology has only continued to grow. To the dismay of my parents I would take apart anything I could get my hands on and occasionally would be able to put it all back together again without any left over components. I quickly realized I was a hardware guy due to the fact I never really understood coding but could read a schematic and probe for voltage before hitting middle school. I started my own computer repair business in my early teens and only stopped when removing viruses and teaching elderly people how to open their email became tedious. I enjoyed playing with circuits and always thought I would become an electrical engineer. However, my true passion has always been flight and I am slowly working my way to becoming a commercial pilot. Between that and a possible future degree in electrical engineering, I would like to one day work for Boeing or Lockheed Martin and design aviation/navigation systems and test them in the field.
